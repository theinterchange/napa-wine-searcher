Wine tasting in Napa and Sonoma can be expensive — some tastings run $100 or more. But plenty of highly-rated wineries offer excellent experiences for under $40. Here are our top picks for value.

## Sonoma County (Generally More Affordable)

### Cline Family Cellars — Sonoma
[Cline](/wineries/cline-cellars) offers tastings starting around $25, with beautiful grounds, a picnic area, and dog-friendly spaces. Their Rhône varietals and old-vine Zinfandel punch well above their price point.

### Viansa Winery — Sonoma
[Viansa's](/wineries/viansa-winery) hilltop setting and Italian-inspired wines come with tastings averaging around $35. The wetland views alone are worth the visit.

### Tank Garage Winery — Calistoga
[Tank Garage](/wineries/tank-garage-winery) offers their seasonal, one-time-only wines at approachable prices — tastings average around $35-40. The retro gas station vibe is unlike anything else in wine country.

### Jacuzzi Family Vineyards — Sonoma
[Jacuzzi](/wineries/jacuzzi-family-vineyards) offers Italian varietal tastings for under $40 with a gorgeous vineyard setting. Walk-ins welcome.

### Korbel Champagne Cellars — Guerneville
[Korbel](/wineries/korbel) is a Sonoma institution with some of the most affordable tastings in wine country. Their champagne cellar tour is free.

## Napa Valley

### V. Sattui Winery — St. Helena
[V. Sattui](/wineries/v-sattui-winery) combines affordable tastings with a full deli and picnic grounds — one of the best value propositions in Napa.

### Monticello Vineyards — Napa
[Monticello](/wineries/monticello-vineyards) in the Stags Leap District offers tastings that are surprisingly affordable for the quality. Their Corley family wines are exceptional.

## How to Save on Wine Tasting

1. **Join wine clubs** — most wineries waive tasting fees for members.
2. **Buy a bottle** — many wineries waive the fee with a purchase.
3. **Visit on weekdays** — some wineries offer lower fees Monday through Thursday.
4. **Look for walk-in options** — wineries that accept walk-ins tend to have lower fees.
5. **Share tastings** — some wineries allow couples to share a single tasting.
