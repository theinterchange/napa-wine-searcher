Sonoma County is one of the most dog-friendly wine regions in California. Many wineries here have sprawling outdoor patios, vineyard paths, and a genuinely welcoming attitude toward well-behaved dogs. Here are our favorites.

## The Best Dog-Friendly Wineries

### St. Francis Winery — Santa Rosa
[St. Francis Winery](/wineries/st-francis-winery) welcomes dogs in their beautiful outdoor tasting areas. With stunning views of the Mayacamas mountains and exceptional Merlot and Cabernet Sauvignon, this is a top pick for both you and your pup.

### Viansa Winery — Sonoma
[Viansa](/wineries/viansa-winery) sits on a hilltop overlooking a wetland preserve — perfect for dogs who love the outdoors. Their Italian-inspired wines and walk-in-friendly policy make it an easy stop.

### Buena Vista Winery — Sonoma
California's oldest premium winery, [Buena Vista](/wineries/buena-vista) has dog-friendly patios amid historic stone buildings. Their Pinot Noir and Chardonnay are excellent.

### Cline Family Cellars — Sonoma
[Cline](/wineries/cline-cellars) has expansive grounds, a picnic area, and some of the most affordable tastings in Sonoma (many under $30). Dogs are welcome throughout the outdoor areas.

### Gundlach Bundschu — Sonoma
One of the oldest family-owned wineries in America, [Gundlach Bundschu](/wineries/gundlach-bundschu) has beautiful grounds for walking with your dog. Their heritage wines are exceptional.

### Kunde Family Winery — Kenwood
[Kunde](/wineries/kunde-family) has 1,850 acres of estate vineyards and plenty of room for dogs to enjoy the scenery. Their mountain-top tasting experiences are among Sonoma Valley's best.

### Benziger Family Winery — Glen Ellen
[Benziger](/wineries/benziger-family) is known for biodynamic farming and their popular tram tours through the vineyards. Dogs are welcome in outdoor areas, and the grounds are gorgeous for a stroll.

### Kendall-Jackson Wine Estate & Gardens — Santa Rosa
[Kendall-Jackson](/wineries/kendall-jackson-wine-estate-gardens) has stunning gardens and outdoor spaces. Dogs are welcome on the grounds, and their food-and-wine pairings are worth the visit.

### Korbel Champagne Cellars — Guerneville
[Korbel](/wineries/korbel) in the Russian River Valley has beautiful gardens and dog-friendly outdoor areas. Their champagne cellar tour is a classic Sonoma experience.

### Frog's Leap Winery — Rutherford
While technically in Napa, [Frog's Leap](/wineries/frogs-leap) is worth mentioning for dog owners. Their organic estate welcomes dogs, and the laid-back atmosphere is quintessential wine country.

## Tips for Wine Tasting with Dogs

- **Call ahead** to confirm current dog policies — they can change seasonally.
- **Bring water and a bowl** — not all wineries have them on hand.
- **Keep dogs leashed** and well-behaved around other guests.
- **Visit in the morning** when it's cooler and less crowded.
- **Plan outdoor tastings** — most dog-friendly areas are patios and gardens, not indoor tasting rooms.
